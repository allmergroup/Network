A Foundry Exchange Experiment · v1.0

Envoy

A physical-first social game. You connect only by meeting in person — every handshake is an Envoice, and the world map colours in as your dispatches grow.

Work in progress

The one idea

In an age of AI-generated everything, human presence is one of the few things that cannot be automated, synthesised, or faked. Envoy turns that into a game.

No remote adds. No usernames to search. No follower counts. The only way to add someone is to stand next to them and both agree. Every connection you have is a person you actually met.

The loop

Meet → Envoice → Stamp → Colour
  1. Meet someone in personAt a café, a campus, a train carriage on an interrail trip. The app gives you a light, low-stakes reason to say hello.
  2. Exchange EnvoicesBoth phones, in proximity, confirm at the same time. An Envoice is filed for both parties — mutual, simultaneous, unfakeable. The friction is the point.
  3. The Envoice stamps your diaryAn animated passport stamp lands. A new dispatch is added to your collection. Their home city is filed.
  4. The globe bursts into watercolourTheir home country shifts from sepia fog to colour. Your dispatches grow. Your reach across the human map expands.

Your dispatches are only the doorway. "Oh, I got a new country" is the hook that starts the conversation — it isn't where the connection ends. The real product is what happens after the stamp: the people you actually keep talking to.

What it is — and isn't

Diary & globe

A 1950s travel-journal aesthetic. Aged paper, typewriter type, passport stamps, and a 3D globe that fills in with watercolour as your reach grows.

Suitcase stickers

No text bios. You pick interest tags that render as retro luggage stickers — Cinephile, Vinyl Collector, Backpacker.

Nomad mode

Not everyone has one home. Nomad mode skips the city lock and travels with you. One tap to switch into it — far easier than changing a fixed Home Base, which stays deliberately hard.

Privacy is paramount

No "a stranger is 250m away." The radar shows only coarse density at a neighbourhood level, never a person's location. Precise location exists only between mutual connections, opt-in and revocable.

Quiet 1:1 chat

A minimal, typewriter-styled message thread with people you've actually met. That's it.

No Instagram lock-in

Cut. The whole pitch is presence that can't be faked; importing a curated online feed works against that. Envoy stands on its own.

How we're treating it

This is an experiment in progress, not a billion-dollar bet. If it grows, it grows. If it dies, that's fine. We seed it the only way a proximity network can work: win one place completely first — a single campus or scene where density is high enough to make the first meetings actually happen — before worrying about the world.

For the programmers — honest tech notes

What's real, what isn't, and what we'd actually build Envoy with.
Read this first. Apple NameDrop / "Share My Profile" is not available to third-party apps. It is a system feature inside Apple's own Contacts app (built on UWB + NFC) with no public API to invoke it. We get the same tap-to-exchange feeling using the frameworks below — we do not depend on NameDrop.

The Envoice mechanic — what actually works

FrameworkRoleReality
MultipeerConnectivityThe exchange. Ad-hoc peer-to-peer over BLE/Wi-Fi, built for nearby device-to-device.Recommended core. Apple-native, no server needed for the swap itself.
Nearby Interaction (UWB)Physical-proximity proof — measures that two phones are inches apart.Great anti-spoof layer. Needs U1/U2 chip (iPhone 11+).
Core Bluetooth (BLE)Broadest-support fallback for the exchange via a custom GATT profile.Works everywhere, more manual than MultipeerConnectivity.
Core NFCTap-style trigger via a tag.Limited. Phone-to-phone NFC isn't really exposed; treat as optional flair, not the mechanism.
Mutual QR + co-located GPSCross-platform / web-honest fallback: both scan, both confirm same place + time.The portable option. Less magical than a tap, but works on any phone and in a browser.

Home Base, anti-spoof & backend

ConcernApproach
Home Base lockCore Location visit / significant-location monitoring inferred over ~2 weeks. Changing it is deliberately hard; switching to Nomad is one tap.
Anti-spoofingApp Attest / DeviceCheck (iOS) and Play Integrity (Android) prove genuine, unmodified app on real hardware. Plus server-side cross-check: both devices must report a matching session nonce + consistent coarse location at Envoice time. UWB distance is the strongest physical proof.
Backend (no DevOps, but yes a backend)A live social graph with presence + realtime is server-side — there's no "serverless" version of that. But you don't have to run servers: a BaaS like Supabase (Postgres + Realtime + Auth + Edge Functions) or Firebase gives auth, database, realtime presence, storage and push without managing infrastructure.
PushAPNs (iOS) / FCM (Android), brokered through the BaaS.
The globe & radarglobe.gl / three.js for the 3D watercolour globe; MapLibre for the coarse density radar. Both run client-side; render the same in native via a webview or rebuild in SceneKit/MapKit.

Native vs. PWA — the blunt verdict

A web app / PWA can do

  • The whole diary, globe, passport, stickers
  • The chat UI
  • This very intro / marketing page
  • QR-based Envoice fallback

It cannot do (the core)

  • Bluetooth/NFC/UWB handshake on iOS — Web Bluetooth & Web NFC don't exist in iOS Safari
  • Background location to lock Home Base
  • Hardware attestation / real anti-spoofing

Recommendation: native, iOS-first (Swift / SwiftUI), or React Native / Flutter with native modules for the Bluetooth, UWB and attestation pieces — paired with a BaaS backend so there's no infrastructure to manage. A PWA is the right tool for the landing page and an optional read-only globe viewer, but it cannot be Envoy. If web is non-negotiable for v1, change the Envoice mechanic to mutual QR + GPS on purpose — that's a product decision, not a workaround to hide.
